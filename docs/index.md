## Why this training exists

Most market-risk and credit-risk training is written for **risk managers** (who know the business and need the math) or for **quants** (who need the models). Almost nothing is written for the **data professional** whose job is to source, model, store, and serve the data that all of the above depend on.

This training fills that gap across both risk disciplines. The focus is always on table structures, grain, dimensions, aggregation rules, and reconciliation — framed inside the business context that makes the answers obvious.

## How to use this site

- **Sequential learners** — pick a track and start at module 01. The Market Risk track is complete; the Credit Risk track is in active development.
- **Reference users** — jump straight to a topic via the top tabs and sidebar, or use search.
- **Quick lookups** — the [glossary](glossary.md) covers terms from both tracks.
- **Planning your time** — see the [Market Risk curriculum](curriculum.md) for per-module hour estimates and three suggested study sequences (sequential beginner, VaR-focused refresher, architecture-focused). The Credit Risk equivalent will follow.
- **Cross-track readers** — several modules are genuinely shared (data-modelling fundamentals, engineering excellence, lineage, bitemporality). The Credit Risk track links back to those Market Risk modules as the canonical home of the topic.

Each module follows the same eight-section structure: **learning objectives**, **why it matters**, **core concepts**, **worked examples** (SQL + Python), **common pitfalls**, **exercises with solutions**, **further reading**, and a **recap**.
